# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Facilitates agile processes, resolves blockers, and promotes best practices in delivery teams. The Scrum Master acts as a servant-leader, coaching the team on agile disciplines and enabling continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Lead standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove team impediments and escalate blockers as needed
- Enable continuous improvement across the delivery team
- Coach the team on agile best practices and self-organization
- Support the Project Manager and serve as a delivery process advocate

### Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates to ensure delivery timelines align with agile cadence; escalates impediments that affect project milestones.
- **Product Manager**: Partners on backlog refinement and sprint goal clarity; ensures stories are well-defined before sprint start.
- **Developers**: Shields from distractions, facilitates ceremonies, and removes blockers to keep the team focused and productive.

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
Drives usability and design alignment with user needs. The UX Designer is the owner of user experience flows, interface design, and usability testing throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create and validate user flows, wireframes, and UI prototypes
- Facilitate usability testing sessions and synthesize feedback
- Ensure accessibility compliance and design consistency
- Maintain a shared design system and component library
- Advocate for user-centered design in all feature decisions

### Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Aligns design deliverables with project milestones and communicates design dependencies.
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on user requirements, personas, and success metrics; translates product goals into user experiences.
- **Developers**: Works closely during implementation to ensure design fidelity and resolve feasibility constraints.

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Ensures documentation is accurate, accessible, and up to date. The Technical Writer is responsible for capturing technical decisions, maintaining user-facing guides, and organizing process documentation across the project.

### Responsibilities
- Capture technical decisions, architecture diagrams, and release notes
- Maintain onboarding materials, user guides, and help documentation
- Edit and organize process docs for clarity and completeness
- Collaborate with engineers to document APIs and integrations
- Review documentation for accuracy, consistency, and accessibility

### Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Keeps project documentation (status reports, decision logs) current and clearly structured.
- **Product Manager**: Documents feature specs, acceptance criteria, and product release notes.
- **Developers**: Partners to document technical designs, API references, and system architecture; reviews PRs for documentation updates.

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
Owns verification and validation of product releases. The QA Engineer leads the definition of testing strategies, drives test automation, and ensures quality gates are met before each release.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test strategies, test plans, and automated test suites
- Advocate for testability in design and implementation
- Perform exploratory, integration, and regression testing
- Track and report defects, coverage metrics, and quality trends
- Collaborate on Definition of Done and acceptance criteria

### Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Reports on quality status and release readiness; surfaces risks that may affect timelines.
- **Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria and ensures delivered features meet defined success metrics.
- **Developers**: Pairs during development for shift-left testing, code review participation, and test automation co-ownership.

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Brings the perspective of business and customer needs to the project. Stakeholders are representatives of key business units or customer groups who provide requirements, participate in key reviews, and serve as decision-makers at critical project milestones.

### Responsibilities
- Participate in project kickoff, milestone reviews, and go/no-go decisions
- Provide and clarify requirements; approve key deliverables
- Raise risks, constraints, and feedback throughout the project lifecycle
- Champion the project within their business unit or customer group
- Balance business priorities with project scope and feasibility

### Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Consulted regularly for status updates, risk decisions, and approval at key gates; participates in retrospectives and launch reviews.
- **Product Manager**: Primary partner for defining product direction, prioritizing features, and validating that outcomes meet business needs.
- **Developers**: Engaged at demos and reviews to provide direct feedback on implemented features.
